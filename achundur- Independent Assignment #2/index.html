How did humans discover fermentation?

Our predecessors took advantage of the naturally-fermented foods available in prehistoric times. Initially, prehistoric humans ate naturally fermented foods for its convenience, and continued to do so because of its myriad health benefits. Humans began deliberately using fermentation thousands of years ago- likely in the Indus valley around 8,000 BCE (Ray 2016, 1).

Learn about fermented foods around the world

Fermented foods make up about one-third of our diets globally (Selhub 2014, 1). Fermentation provides a source of safely-preserved food during times of scarcity, is easily accessible for all classes, and over time, fermentation has become a frequently used tool in many cuisines.
